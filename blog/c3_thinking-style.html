Growth Mindset and Phase 0 So Far.

Hard Things are Hard

I'm not 25 anymore but if I could tell that guy something it would be this:

There's Not Liking Something becasue you don't like it.

And there's not liking it becasue ITS HARD RIGHT NOW.

This idea, I think encapsulates a growth mindset. It pre-supposes that change is possible, it even reminds us that while it may not be fun, it will be worthwhile.

I do not like Chinese Opera. I have relatives who perform it and I have seen it and let me tell you, it is a real challenge. I don't speak Mandarin, its not my culture in the first place, it has nothing in common with anythign I do like and I just have no desire to become someone who would like it anyway.

I don't think it is BAD or EVIL or STUPID. I don't think IT SHOULDN'T EXIST. It's just not my cup of tea. I just don't like it very much

I'm trying to set this up as an example of "I just don't like it".

Skiing, driving stick, a musical instrument, small talk at parties with strangers ..learning to do these things absolutely sucked. But they where totally worthwile.

It was hard, and at the time, I was really not enjoying it. I did not like it. But it passed.

First Three Weeks

Its the little things

Keeping all that in mind, I'm not sure how I feel about HTML and CSS. I think its just frustrating that I'm not very good at it. This will pass and I will get better.

The Ruby stuff in week three was such a relief. First, you aren't so freaked out about design and visual appeal, just getting the answer. There is no visual interpretation to "which one is the shortest string in the array".

But its the small things. A part of me wants to focus on the BIG STUFF. Doing the Challenges.

But there is rather a lot of small stuff. Getting everythign up on Git, getting your web site to point correctly, doing reflections, the final submission on Socrates, arranging Pairing, god god.

all that adds up to a lot of time when you aren't working on the big stuff.

But I am sure that will pass.

But Chinese opera.. no way man.
