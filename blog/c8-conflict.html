When I was first interviewed for DBC, it was last March, they were moving to Google Hangouts from Skype.

For my interview, that I had to wait, like 5 weeks to get, I was going to be asked questions about my current ruby knowledge, given some puzzles to solve and chat, I guess. Interview stuff.

It was set for 10:30am on a Thursday morning. The interviewer was.. I forget his name but I Google-stalked the hell out of him.

I studied my "Learn to Program" and I did my Code School excercises and I read the appropriate chapters in "Search Inside Yourself", I even started meditating. About 5 - 10 minutes a day when I wasn't too busy, so maybe 3-5 days a week.

I was nervous as hell becasue I really wanted to do this. One way or another; get through DBC and your direction in this world is severly changed. And I have found as an adult that when I care about things they make we very nervous.

Thurday comes and I'm vibrating with butterflies, quite possibly litterally shaking. Trying to calm down, just not able to.

But eventually its 10:20. The call is in 10 minutes. I have been clearly instructed that the call will be on skype and that I should keep skype open becasue of .. reasons...

Anyway, becasue I have google stalked this poor interviewer I know he has interviews all morning, so of course he will run late.

So at 10:31 When I have heard nothing, I do not panic. I am cool, I am not some kind of Hot-Head. I am exactly the person they want for this program. No email, no skype, no worries. He is busy and he will get back to me when he is ready.

So at 10:40 when I still haven't heard anything, I am, well appalled to see two new emails.
1. You missed your interview and you application to Dev Bootcamp is being disregarded.
2. An earlier email: Here is the link to the Google Hangout for your interview.

Now I would like to tell you that I took three measured breaths and found my center and thought silently, "oh well".

That is not what happened. I flipped the f- out.

And as quick as I could, I installed the Google Hangout thing, and called him back.

And that is when something miraculous happened.

Instead of the interviewer picking up the Hangout and me SCREAMING at him:

"You said SKYPE!
I have been waiting Months!
I am NOT starting over!
I will go someplace else you "so-and-so"! etc..

no no.
Insted a tiny voice in my head pipped up and said, what if we try a different tone. Perhaps apologetic?

And that is when Karim Bishay, (not the interviewer I had been Google-stalking), picked up the hangout. And I put on a big smile and I .. apologized.

"I'm so sorry, I thought we were going to do this on Skype, I just saw the link to Google hangout" All self-recriminatory smiles.
Perhaps the greatest performance of my career.

He was, as I suspect he always is, totally cool about it. No problem. We did the interview, I made jokes, he laughed, I think genuinely.

I was not a complete idiot.

I got out of the interview feeling that it went as well as it possibly could.

Afterwards I had so much pent up nervous energy I almost fainted.

I have told this story about 20 times.
Some people believe that the Skype/Google mix up is actually an elaborate psychological test performed by Dev Bootcamp.

I remain skeptical.

But the one thing I know, with a fervent religious faith is: if I gone with outrage, instead of a more measured response, I, absolutely, would not be here now.
There is a area of you brain, in the frontal cortex, that is responsible for impulse control.
It has been shown to react positively to meditation.
That little voice in my head saved my ass.
Maybe it was the meditation.
